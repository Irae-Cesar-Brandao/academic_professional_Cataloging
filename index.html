Academic Cataloging Application for Researchers (ACAR)

Iraê César Brandão - Web Developer - Updated on: 03/23/2026

Purpose of Use:

This framework was developed to structure, organize, and problematize academic production from a relational perspective of knowledge, in which references and citations are not merely technical records but expressions of epistemological and institutional connections. By integrating bibliographic data and citation practices within a single environment, it aims not only to facilitate research systematization but also to highlight the dynamics of merit construction, authorship, and knowledge circulation in the academic field.

System Usage Guide

Step Action Description Attention ⚠️
1 Add Reference Creates a new block for bibliographic data entry. Fill all relevant fields before saving.
2 Add Citation Add one or more citations linked to the reference. Avoid leaving citation fields empty.
3 Save Stores data in browser (localStorage). Required before exporting or clearing.
4 Update Edits an existing selected record. Only works after clicking "Edit".
5 Export XML Generates structured file for future import. Ideal for full backup.
6 Export XLS Exports data for spreadsheets. Citations are grouped in one field.
7 Export PDF Generates formatted report. Non-editable format.
8 Import XML Restores or merges previous data. Check duplicates before merging.
9 Clear Deletes all data. ⚠️ Always export before ("irreversible action)".

Citation Standards and Models

✅ ABNT (NBR 10520):(Brazilian Association of Technical Standards)
Uses an author-date system. Example: (LAST NAME, 2020, p. 10). Requires complete identification of the work in the reference.
(e.g., LAST NAME, Author's First Name. Article Title. Journal Title, Publication Location, Volume, Number, Initial-Final Pages, Month Apr. Year. DOI or Available at: URL. Accessed on: Day Month Apr. Year).

✅ SBC:(Brazilian Computer Society)
Based on author-year, similar to ABNT, but with variations in reference formatting. Used in scientific articles in the field of computing.

✅ MLA (9th edition): (Modern Language Association)
For scientific, academic, or journal articles, it focuses on the precise location of the material. In the text, use:

  • Pattern: (Last Name Page) - e.g., (Poythress 185).
  • Narrative: According to Poythress... (185).
  • Two authors: (Almeida and Azevedo 45).
  • Three or more authors: (Latartara et al. 97).
  • References: Last Name, First Name. "Article Title." Journal Name...

✅ AMA :(American Medical Association)
Numerical system. Citations appear as numbers in the text.
(e.g., [1]). The reference list is ordered according to appearance. Essential data includes author, title, journal, volume, and year).

✅ Vancouver:(Vancouver- Health and Medicine)
It also uses a numerical system. Very common in the health field. References follow the order of citation in the text.
(e.g., (1), (2), etc.)

✅ Types of Citation: (Bibliography and References)

  • Short Direct citation: up to 3 lines, in citation marks.
  • Long Direct citation: more than 3 lines, indented.
  • Indirect citation: paraphrase.
  • Apud: citation of a citation.

Fundamental data for citation: Author, year, page, title of the work and context of the citation.

Recommendations on Ethics and Rights Author's Note

🚫 Avoid Plagiarism: Every thought, data, or phrase that is not your own must be referenced:

  • Integrity: Ensures honesty in the representation of another person's ideas.
  • Respect: Demonstrates appreciation for the intellectual contributions of others.
  • Responsibility:Allows your work to be traced and verified through its sources.
  • Academic Reputation: Maintains your academic credibility and integrity.
